[August 2021 - December 2021]

Epidural simulator designed to train nurses at Duke School of Nursing consisting of skin layer, adjustable fat layer, CAD spine, and fluid feedback tubing

  • Skills: Fusion360 (CAD), Meshmixer

Existing Solutions

      Current epidural simulators are incredibly expensive and do not last that long due to fluid leakage from the tubing. Puncture holes can be seen through the skin which makes it easy for nursing students to feel their way into the right spot. In addition, there is only model simulator with a protruding spine and no fat causing it to not be representative of patients receiving epidurals.

Task

    • Design new epidural simulator for Duke Nursing School
    • Realistic feeling skin and fat layers
    • Anatomically correct spine
    • Adjustable fat layers for different BMI patients
    • Minimize leakage in epidural space
    • Cost effective solution

Presentation

CAD Spine

Using Fusion360 and MeshMixer, a durable, anatomically correct spine was 3D printed using PLA to accurately represent the spine and epidural space near the spinal cord

Skin/Fat Molding

Using modeling foams, fat layers of varying thickness were produced to represent patients of different BMIs. The skin layer was molded by Dragon Skin and other silicones and colored with makeup concealer in order to look and feel like human skin. The skin was also resealable so no puncture holes were visible

Pressurized Tubing

Latex surgical tubing was used as it is most durable to punctures and worked well being pressurized with 18 cm H20 so that the correct rate of fluid feedback was achieved by the epidural
